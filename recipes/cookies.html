Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

This reduced fat and sugar recipe makes just as chewy delicious chocolate chip cookies as the original*. As always make sure to skim the recipe before you start. Happy Baking!

* this recipe is modified from the original

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup White Sugar
  • 7 and 1/2 tbsp Light Brown Sugar, packed
  • 1/4 tsp Salt
  • 1/4 cup Unsalted Butter, melted
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 tsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1 cup and 3 tbsp All Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 3.5 oz Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chunks

Steps

  1. Whisk sugar, salt, and butter into a paste.
  2. Whisk in egg and vanilla, beat until light ribbons fall off the whisk and remain for a short while before falling sinking back into the mixture.
  3. Sift in flour and baking soda, being careful not to overmix, fold the mixture with a spatula until a dough forms. (Not overmixing is the key to chewy cookies!)
  4. Finally fold in the chocolate chunks, and chill the dough for 30 minutes, or overnight for a more toffee like flavor.
  5. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F.
  6. Form 16 equally sized cookie dough balls and place them each roughly 4 inches apart.
  7. Bake for 8-12 minutes. Serve hot or cooled!
